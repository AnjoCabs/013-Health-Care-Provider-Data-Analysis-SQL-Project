# 013-Health-Care-Provider-Data-Analysis-SQL-Project

🏥 Hospital Patient & Healthcare Analytics Using SQL
📌 Project Overview

This project demonstrates SQL-based exploratory data analysis (EDA) on a hospital management dataset. The objective is to analyze patient visits, healthcare revenue, provider performance, insurance coverage, patient satisfaction, referral patterns, and operational efficiency using real-world business questions.

🎯 Project Objectives
Analyze patient volume and hospital growth.
Evaluate departmental and provider performance.
Measure hospital revenue and insurance contributions.
Assess patient satisfaction across departments and providers.
Identify common diagnoses and high-cost treatments.
Examine emergency service utilization.
Analyze insurance coverage and payment status.
Understand referral sources and patient demographics.
Generate healthcare KPIs for operational decision-making.

📊 Business Questions Solved
👥 Patient Volume & Growth Analysis

1. Monthly patient visits
Tracks patient volume over time to identify growth trends.

2. Departments with the highest patient volume
Identifies departments serving the most patients.

3. Cities generating the most patients
Analyzes where patients are coming from.

💰 Revenue & Financial Performance

4. Revenue generated by each department
Measures departmental financial performance.

5. Highest revenue-generating medical procedures
Determines which procedures contribute the most revenue.

6. Percentage of revenue covered by insurance
Evaluates the hospital's reliance on insurance reimbursements.

7. Insurance providers contributing the most revenue
Ranks insurance companies based on revenue generated.

😊 Patient Satisfaction Analysis

8. Departments with the highest satisfaction scores
Measures patient satisfaction across hospital departments.

9. Highest-rated healthcare providers
Identifies providers delivering the best patient experiences.

10. Relationship between treatment cost and satisfaction
Examines whether treatment cost influences patient satisfaction.

👨‍⚕️ Provider Performance Analysis

11. Providers handling the most patients
Measures provider workload.

12. Providers generating the highest revenue
Evaluates provider financial performance.

🩺 Diagnosis & Disease Analysis

13. Most common diagnoses
Identifies frequently treated medical conditions.

14. Diagnoses generating the highest treatment costs
Highlights the most expensive diagnoses to treat.

🚑 Emergency Service Analysis

15. Percentage of emergency visits
Measures the share of emergency-related patient visits.

16. Diagnoses most associated with emergency visits
Determines which conditions most frequently require emergency care.

🏥 Insurance Analysis

17. Insurance providers covering the most patients
Ranks insurance companies by patient volume.

18. Insurance providers with the highest unpaid claims
Identifies insurers associated with the largest outstanding balances.

📈 Referral & Patient Demographics

19. Referral sources generating the most patients
Determines the most effective patient acquisition channels.

20. Referral sources generating the highest revenue
Measures revenue contribution by referral source.

21. Healthcare spending by age group
Analyzes spending patterns across different age categories.

22. Average treatment cost by gender
Compares healthcare costs between genders.

23. Percentage of visits with unpaid balances
Measures the hospital's outstanding payment rate.

🔍 Advanced Healthcare Analysis

24. High-cost diagnoses with low patient satisfaction
Identifies treatment areas requiring quality improvement.

25. Departments with high patient volume and low satisfaction
Highlights departments that may need operational improvements or additional resources.

⭐ Key Takeaways

This project demonstrates how SQL can transform healthcare data into meaningful business insights that improve patient care, financial management, and operational efficiency. Through multi-table joins, conditional aggregations, subqueries, KPI reporting, and healthcare-focused analytics, it showcases practical SQL skills applicable to Healthcare Data Analyst, Business Intelligence Analyst, Data Analyst, and SQL Developer roles.
